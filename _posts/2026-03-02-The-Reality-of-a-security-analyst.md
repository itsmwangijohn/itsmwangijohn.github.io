## Content Preview:

My path as a Security Analyst wasn't built in a classroom; it was forged in the labs of ** TryHackMe** and **Hack The Box**. There is a unique rush in the "initial foothold"that moment when a vulnerability is discovered, a script is executed, and the user flag is captured.

#### My Hands-on Expertise:
* Vulnerability Research: Navigating through complex rooms on THM to master the OWASP Top 10, moving from basic SQL injection to advanced Buffer Overflows.
* The HTB Grind: Tackling retired and active machines on Hack The Box, where "Try Harder" is a mantra. This has sharpened my skills in enumeration, privilege escalation, and lateral movement.
* lag Chasing as Training: Every flag captured represents a solved puzzle. Whether it’s pivoting through a network or bypassing a poorly configured WAF, these labs simulate the high-pressure environment of real-world incident response.

### Why Labs Matter for an Analyst:

In a SOC environment, you cannot defend what you do not understand. By spending hours in pentesting labs, I’ve learned to recognize the subtle "footprints" attackers leave behind. I don't just see a log entry; I see the exploit attempt behind it.
